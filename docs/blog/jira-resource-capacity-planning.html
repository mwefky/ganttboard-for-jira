Resource & capacity planning in Jira, free

Jira tells you who a task is assigned to. It does not tell you whether that person is overloaded. You can have one teammate buried under five overlapping tasks while another sits idle, and nothing on the board flags it — until standup, or until a deadline slips. Resource and capacity planning closes that gap, and you don't need an enterprise suite to get the essentials.

The problem with capacity in plain Jira

Assignees are a field, not a workload model. To know if someone is over capacity, you have to mentally sum every task assigned to them across overlapping dates and compare it to the days they actually work that week. Nobody does that reliably by hand, so overallocation stays invisible until it bites.

The heavy options

The enterprise answer is a full resource module — BigPicture, or the resource features in Structure.Gantt — with capacity histograms, allocation percentages, and team-level planning. They're powerful and priced accordingly, and they're more than most teams need just to answer "who's drowning this week?"

The lean, free way

GanttBoard for Jira ships a focused Capacity view (Velocity Mode) that answers exactly that question, free:

  • A one-line banner at the top: who is over capacity this week, and on which days.
  • Each assignee's workload across the timeline, color-coded — green when they're fine, amber near capacity, red when overloaded.
  • Click a person to see the exact tasks driving their load, so you can reassign or reschedule to rebalance — and each task opens straight in Jira.

It reads your existing Jira assignees and dates, respects your working days and holidays, and needs no setup. To open it, click Capacity in the GanttBoard toolbar.

How to use it day to day

  1. Open Capacity on Monday. If the banner is green, you're balanced. If it's red, it names who's over and when.
  2. Click an overloaded person to see their tasks for the window.
  3. Drag a task to a different date, or reassign it, until the banner goes green.

What it is — and isn't

This is deliberately the lean version: it surfaces overallocation and lets you rebalance fast. It is not a full capacity-planning suite with allocation percentages, cost, or cross-program portfolio views — if you need those, BigPicture is the category leader. But for a small Jira team that just wants to stop people quietly drowning, the free Capacity view covers the 80% that actually matters.
